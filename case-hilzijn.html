Hilzijn Rebranding
Campagne

A care group is judged on trust long before anyone reads a word. For Zorggroep Hilzijn I rebuilt that first impression around the people they actually help — turning real client journeys into illustrated portraits, and rolling them out from Instagram to bus stops.

01 — The concept

The story behind the face

Care marketing usually hides behind stock photography and soft language. We went the other way: every campaign asset starts with one real person and one real journey. Illustrated portraits keep the story recognisable while protecting the client's privacy — honest, but never exposed.

“Verhalen samenbrengen is onze kunst.”

02 — The portraits

Six journeys, six faces

Each story got its own illustrated portrait and written account — from Yassin, who travelled to a new country alone, to Bertus, who rebuilt his independence at 68. The palette shifts per person, but the frame, type and tone stay identical, so the set reads as one family.

03 — Digital

Built for the feed

The portraits were designed to survive a thumb at speed: a strong face, a single colour field, and the campaign line locked to the same position every time. The grid becomes a gallery of people rather than a wall of posts.

04 — Out of home

From screen to street

A campaign about being seen shouldn't stay online. The same portrait system scales from a bus-stop poster to a full bus wrap, a building banner and the company vans — one visual language, wherever it lands.

05 — Print & merchandise

Something to carry with you

Business cards, art prints, tees and mugs turn the campaign into something staff and residents can hold — and quietly extend the brand well past the campaign period.

06 — The mark

One promise holding it together

Every touchpoint closes on the same lockup and the same line — the monogram, the handwritten wordmark, and a promise the whole campaign is built to prove.

“De zorggroep die inzet op authenticiteit.”
