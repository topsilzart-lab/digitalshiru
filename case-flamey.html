Case Study · UI & UX Design

Flamey —
Smart Heating

Heating dashboards are usually a wall of numbers. Flamey turns a home's gas use into something you can read at a glance — room by room, month by month — so saving energy feels less like homework and more like a nudge.

ProductFlamey · Smart heating
DisciplineUI & UX design · Branding
ScopeDashboard · Analytics · Logo
PlatformWeb & mobile
01 — The approach

A thoughtfully constructed experience

The interface was built in greyscale first. Structure, hierarchy and rhythm had to hold up with no colour at all — only then did the flame palette go on top. It's the quickest way to find out whether a layout is actually working or just looking busy.

02 — The interface

Sleek and intuitive design

Clean, minimal aesthetics that make navigation simple and enjoyable. Every feature is a couple of taps away, whether you're adjusting a temperature or setting a schedule — and the numbers that matter are always the biggest thing on the card.

“Clarity first. Colour second.”

03 — Personalised zones

Your home, room by room

Flamey's room and zone views are designed to be instantly recognisable, giving users a clear overview of their home's heating at a glance. The ECO Blueprint maps usage straight onto the floorplan, so a cold room isn't a statistic — it's a place.

04 — Identity

A logo that flows like a flame

The mark folds warm and cool into a single flame — gas on one side, savings on the other. Built on a strict grid so it stays legible from a favicon up to a full-screen splash.

05 — Across devices

Same system, any screen

The card-based layout collapses cleanly from desktop to phone: the same widgets, restacked. Nothing is hidden on mobile — it just queues up.
